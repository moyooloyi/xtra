Interest on a savings account is the amount of money a bank or financial institution pays a depositor for holding their money with the bank. When you earn interest in a savings account, the bank is literally paying you money to keep your cash deposited there.

Savings accounts earn compound interest, which means the interest you earn in one period gets deposited into your account, and then in the next period, you earn interest on that interest.

The largest advantage of paying interest is it is a relatively low expense compared to alternatives.
